The Legendary Career of Cristiano Ronaldo

Early Years & Sporting CP (2002-2003)

Ronaldo's professional journey began at Sporting Clube de Portugal in Lisbon. After rising through the youth ranks, he made his first-team debut at the age of 17. His standout performance against Manchester United in August 2003 convinced Sir Alex Ferguson to immediately sign the talented winger.

Manchester United | First Era (2003-2009)

Joining United for a record fee for a teenager, Ronaldo inherited the iconic No. 7 shirt. This period saw his transformation from a promising winger into a global superstar. Key achievements included:

  • Winning 3 consecutive Premier League titles (2007, 2008, 2009).
  • Securing the UEFA Champions League in 2008.
  • Winning his first Ballon d'Or in 2008.

Juventus (2018-2021) | Conquering Italy

Ronaldo became the first player to win league titles in England, Spain, and Italy by leading Juventus to back to back Serie A titles. He maintained a prolific scoring rate, winning the Capocannoniere (Serie A top scorer) in his final season.

Later Career (2021-Present)

He returned to Manchester United for a season and a half, scoring 24 goals in his first season back. In January 2023, he joined Saudi Pro League side Al-Nassr FC, where he continues to compete and break records, becoming the league's top scorer and winning the Arab Club Champions Cup in his debut year.

International Career: Portugal

As captain of the Portuguese national team, Ronaldo is the most-capped male player and the all-time leading goalscorer in international football history. His greatest successes with the national team include:

  • UEFA European Championship winner (Euro 2016)
  • UEFA Nations League winner (2019)
